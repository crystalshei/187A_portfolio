Theme: THE BODY

Concept: dealing with the sequential nature of time. Linearity vs. Continuity

Materials: hemp string, red and white cardstock, glue

Abstract:

My piece discusses the concept of time, and the event as an individual body within that conceptual space. I play with the idea of segments in time as bodies. There's a generally accepted idea that time is linear, hence the word "timeline". As such, events, as individual entities in time, are perceived to have an A to B structure. However, I disagree with this simplistic linear structure. I think that there's a more complex relational nature to time and to historical events. With my piece, I have two different layers of temporal relationship: first, the events themselves, and then, the events in relation to each other. Each small strip, curved into a mobius strip, is an event. They are linked with one or more of the other strips. The strips being curved in on themselves in a circular (literally and figuratively) manner symbolize the way in which events drive themselves forward. As they are linked up with other strips, there's a bigger picture than just the isolated event. The different colors of the strips just show that there are different types of events, or bodies.
